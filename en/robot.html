R.O.B.O.T

The Mari 51 intel was able to find the plans for the robot that the raiders are planning to use for the raid. The Base decided to rebuild this robot to be able to better understand the raiders’ minds.

What does the robot do?
This robot was designed to be very fast and easy to manoeuver, which is why the H-Drive system is used. Each actuator in the field has a corresponding mechanism on the robot. For the mushrooms, the arm is extended to hit the buttons, for the arch, the flipper winds up and hits the carriage, for the ball and chain, the robot backs up into the actuator on the side of its slope, for the hinged board, either the arm or an extending piece from the robot will hit it, and for the twister, the robot can insert a flap of the actuator inside of its frame to control it from both directions.

The Drivebase
The H-Drive system with omni wheels allows for holonomic drive, which is great for driving around the field as fast as possible. We used four 1:20 gearboxes with RS550 motors and a central 1:26 gearbox with an RS555 motor. This lets us drive very fast and be able to handle the root easily. While building however, we had to keep weight distribution in mind to be able to properly drive. One thing that did not go as expected for the base was the fact that the central wheel did not always touch the ground. To fix this, we brought down the extrusion the motor was attached to.

The Arm
This arm is meant to extend out of the robot to touch the furthest mushroom actuator. It uses three VEX motors and four limit switches. One motor brings the arm into the up position or down to extend either into or out of the robot. The other two motors rotate the arm so that it can extend into the robot for driving around or outside the robot for hitting the mushroom. The arm can also be used to activate the hinged board and to drag the carriage across the arch. One thing that we struggled with was finding a light enough rod to rotate that would not make the torque too great.

The Flipper
The current flipper mechanism consists of a piece of extrusion around an axle that is attached to a string on one end. The string winds up around a large wheel. The reason for this wheel is that one rotation is not enough for the motor to reach max speed. With the string, the wheel can be rotating while winding up the string until it is taught. Then, the string’s tension will force the flipper piece to flick upwards with a lot of force. The big struggle with this mechanism is making sure that the string does not get tangled anywhere outside the wheel. Currently, we are using a cage to trap in the string to the wheel. This mechanism, however, may be subject to change since the raiders are working on an alternative.

The Twister
For the twister actuator, two vertical pieces of extrusion were added to the side of the robot. With this, the robot can drive into the actuator and insert the flap in between the two pieces of extrusion. This will allow for the robot to be able to move the actuator front and back. This mechanism was placed on the side of the robot instead of on the front or back so that the robot does not get easily pushed because of its omni wheels.

The Ball & Chain
The robot’s ball and chain mechanism consists of a slope made of of VEX metal that matches the slope of the actuator. This slope has a bit of a dent in it so that the ball can be held. The idea is that the robot drives towards the actuator and the slope of the robot presses the ball towards the actuator’s sensor.

The Hinged Board
For the hinged board, in order to get to it first, we used very fast gearboxes and motor combinations. We will also extend the arm outwards so that we have an extra three feet to hit the board with.
