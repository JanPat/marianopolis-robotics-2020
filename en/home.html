The Marianopolis Robotics Team

The Marianopolis Robotics Team is a multidisciplinary club made up of individuals who are passionate about mechanical and electrical engineering, computer science, art, construction, web design, and filmmaking. They compete in the annual CRC Robotics Competition against high schools and CEGEPS across Canada.

This year the Marianopolis Robotics Team is made up of 18 students and their mentor, Dominique Paradis. At the beginning of each year, over 80 students apply and go through a selection process to get on the team. Any Marianopolis student can be accepted, no matter their experience or program of study. A new team is formed every fall to replace the graduating members from the year before.

By joining Marianopolis Robotics, each member must dedicate their time and knowledge to the team. The team meets up during breaks between their classes as well as most of the winter break. The members each have a variety of talents ranging from coding and construction to music and graphic design. For this reason, the team places members in subteams so that anyone on the team can help with whatever they please.

The members of the team work very hard on all aspects of the competition and show immense passion for their work throughout the season. The team also prides itself in its sense of community and mentorship; older members often mentor and teach the newer members the tricks and skills they will then pass on the next year.

The Canadian Robotics Competition

The Canadian Robotics Competition, CRC, is a nonprofit organization that runs an annual robotics competition. It was founded in 2001 and is currently run by former participants of the competition. They encourage and inspire participants’ expanding knowledge in computer science, robotics, arts, design, and much more. This is possible because the CRC Robotics Competition is not just an ordinary robotics competition. Participants also have to design and create a kiosk to house the team during the competition, a website to show the team’s work, a video, as well as tutorials to help teach other teams the techniques used during the competition. This multidisciplinary competition thus allows for a lot of room for inclusion and introduces a lot of people to different fields.

Every year, the game of the CRC Robotics Competition is different. Past year competitions include Loops, Converto, and Vertigoal. Another great part about CRC is that the nature of the competitions encourages teamwork between teams. This ultimately leads to a better learning experience for everyone involved.
