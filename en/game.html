FLIP 2020

Due to the secretive nature of the Mari 51 base, a few have grown suspicious of our activities. The Mari 51 intelligence committee found that a raid of the base is being planned by a group of curious and reckless alien enthusiasts. Fortunately, they are amateurs and did not protect their raiding plans online. Their plan is called Flip 2020 and they want to storm the alien laboratory containing numerous alien artefacts collected throughout the years. According to their plans, four specialized robots, each belonging to a different raiding team, will enter the lab sometime from February 13 to February 15 2020.

Here is a transcript from a message sent by the person who is organizing the raid:

Good evening fellow alien truth seekers,

This message will detail our mission and strategies for successfully raiding one of the Mari 51 laboratories and proving to the world that aliens exist.

To do this, we will need to get our hands on as many alien artefacts as possible. Our source informed us that the base keeps most of their artefacts inside of a room that is completely inaccessible. Luckily, we can control the conveyor and artefact dispenser that are in the room by interacting with different actuators in a neighboring lab room. Once the artefacts are on the conveyor, we want them to land in our collection bucket, which will either be coloured yellow or blue.

We will have 5 minutes to collect as many items as possible. The artefacts are put inside red or black protective sleeves that look like beanbags. Our source told us that the black beanbags have more precious items. To put this into perspective, if the red items are worth 20 points, the black ones would be worth 200 points. Our group of raiders partnered with another group that has a robot and we agreed to share our artefacts as long as we trigger at least one actuator. However, we will have to compete as a team against two rival teams with robots who are also looking to collect as many artefacts as possible. You will meet these people on the day of the raid and you’ll be able to tell who’s who by the colour of their robot.

As I said before, you can control the conveyor and artefact dispenser from the neighboring lab. The conveyor switches direction every 30 seconds. This is called a conveyor period. There will be 5 different types of actuators in the lab: the chained ball, the hinged board, the mushrooms, the twister, and the arch. Each one affects either the conveyor or dispenser in a unique way. We will call this an actuator power-up. While a power-up is active, a green light will blink. If a red light is being shown, that means the actuator is in its downtime and the power-up cannot be activated. Here is a rundown on what each actuator does:

THE MUSHROOM ACTUATOR
This system consists of two separate mushroom actuators placed at a certain distance from the game field. When the closer mushroom is pressed, the speed of the conveyor is 1.5x its normal speed. When the further one is pressed, the speed of the conveyor is doubled. This power-up lasts for 20 seconds.



THE HINGED BOARD
This board must be pressed to start the conveyor. If this board is pressed, it will immediately flip the direction of the conveyor and start a new conveyor period 1 minute after the initial conveyor start up. This conveyor may only be used once.



THE CHAINED BALL
When the switch is pressed by the ball, the drop rate of the dispenser will double. This power-up lasts 20 seconds.



THE TWISTER
Unlike the others, this actuator is common to both teams. If the flap of the actuator is perpendicular to the conveyor belt for an uninterrupted 3 seconds, the conveyor belt will stop moving for 10 seconds.



THE ARCH
To activate this actuators power-up, the carriage on the arch must be brought from side to side. Before entering the lab, we will get to choose the power-up associated to the arch actuator. 19. Our choices are to release one artefact in a black beanbag, halve the speed of the conveyor for 20 seconds, or increase the drop rate by 3x for 20 seconds.



We’ll try to be as careful as possible when we send our robot into the lab. We have to make sure we do not touch the other team’s actuators as well as damage anything in the lab. Our robot will start on one end of the lab, near the ball and chain, and on the other side of the hinged board. You can see a floor plan of the actuator lab below:



On the day of the raid, we will send in one driver and one co-pilot to control the robot. Meanwhile, we will raid another lab in the Mari 51 base that we can access. Once the raid is done, we will all meet up at homebase and show the world the truth about extraterrestrial life.

I want to believe,
~ Ioanna
