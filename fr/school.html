The Mari 51 Secret Alien Base

The secret Mari 51 Base is disguised as Marianopolis College, a private CEGEP located at 4873 Westmount Ave, Westmount, Quebec H3Y 1X9 since 2007. While the college was founded in 1908, the base has only been at its current location for almost 13 years. With its twelve wings, various science and computer laboratories, and its proximity to the Vendome and Villa Maria metro stations, this location is perfect for the undercover base.

While the college is used for base research in extraterrestrial matters, it is also a fully functioning CEGEP where over 2100 students study. It offers various pre-university programs that last two to three years in the sciences, arts, and social sciences. These programs include Health Sciences, Pure and Applied Sciences, Arts and Science, Liberal Arts, Social Sciences and Music. Honours options are available for some of these programs as well.

Marianopolis is also the only college that offers six certificates focusing on different social themes that any student can earn before graduating. These certificates are the environmental studies certificate, the gender sex/uality certificate, the international studies certificate, the law and social justice certificate, the native studies certificate, and the third world studies certificate. Students can earn these certificates through specific course work and other extracurricular activities.

The students of the school are also encouraged to participate in clubs - Marianopolis offers over 150 different clubs and athletic teams. Some of the clubs, such as the Marianopolis Robotics Club, serve as a cover for Mari 51 Base operations. Other popular clubs at Marianopolis include the Programming Club, Silver Key, and Model UN.

Activities and Events

To maintain its cover as a normal CEGEP, Marianopolis often hosts many events throughout the academic year for its students. Here are some of them:

MariHacks

MariHacks is an 24-hour long hackathon hosted by Marianopolis. It is exclusively for CEGEP and high school students. For a whole day, hackers will collaborate on a software project and pitch it to a panel of judges. The hackathon welcomes everyone, no matter their programming experience. There are also mentors present during the day that will help teams out with their projects and teach people valuable lessons about technology and entrepreneurship.

MariMUN

MariMUN is a student-run club where its members participate in various Model United Nations conferences in Montreal and, for a select few, around the world. At these conferences members represent different countries or characters as they tackle different discussion and debate topics. The club hosts a week-end conference each year for high school and CEGEP students. MariMUN is a great experience where people develop their public speaking skills and are opened up to a world of law, politics, business, and much more.
